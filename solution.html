Waste-to-Energy Composting System

Urban apartment complexes generate a significant amount of food waste, which often ends up in landfills. This not only increases pollution but also wastes valuable resources. To tackle this problem, the proposed Waste-to-Energy Composting System for Apartments offers an eco-friendly solution that converts food waste into both renewable energy and useful compost. This system reduces food waste, cuts energy costs, and helps the environment.

Step 1: Collection

Food waste is collected from each apartment in a dedicated bin system to ensure proper segregation.

Step 2: Anaerobic Digestion

The collected waste undergoes anaerobic digestion, breaking down food scraps without oxygen to produce biogas.

Step 3: Biogas Generation

The biogas is captured and used as a renewable energy source to power shared spaces or apartment utilities.

Step 4: Compost Production

The leftover material, called digestate, is turned into nutrient-rich compost for gardening and landscaping.
