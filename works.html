How It Works

The Waste-to-Energy system uses anaerobic digestion to convert food waste into renewable energy and nutrient-rich compost, providing a sustainable solution to urban waste management challenges.

Step 1: Anaerobic Digestion

Food waste is mixed with water in a sealed container. Microorganisms break it down in the absence of oxygen, producing biogas.

Step 2: Biogas Storage

The biogas, mainly methane, is stored in a pressurized tank. This gas is ready to be used as a renewable energy source.

Step 3: Energy Generation

Biogas powers a generator to produce electricity or heat for common areas, reducing energy costs for the residents.

Step 4: Compost Production

The leftover digestate is turned into compost, which can be used for gardening, farming, or beautifying the community.
